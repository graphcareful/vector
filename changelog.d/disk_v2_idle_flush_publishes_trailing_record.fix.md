The `disk_v2` buffer no longer strands the last record written before it goes idle. The buffer's writer only flushes buffered writes to the reader when a write overflows its internal buffer or when the periodic idle-flush task runs, and that idle flush previously did nothing unless there were finalizers awaiting acknowledgement. A record carrying no finalizers — the common case for the final record of a batch — was therefore left buffered and unread until some later write happened to force a flush; if no further writes arrived (for example, once upstream traffic stopped after a fault), the record could sit undelivered indefinitely even though it was safely on disk. The idle flush now publishes any buffered write to the reader regardless of finalizer state, so a trailing record is made readable and its reader notified within one flush interval.
