Fixed a `disk_v2` buffer losing an end-to-end-acknowledged event when a sink was torn down with a delivery still in flight (for example, during a graceful shutdown). The record's finalizer was dropped without an acknowledgement, which the buffer treated as a successful delivery and advanced its read position past the record, so it was skipped and lost on restart. The read position now only advances for records that were genuinely delivered; a record whose delivery was dropped without acknowledgement is retained and re-delivered when the buffer is reopened. This matches the existing behavior on a hard crash, where nothing is acknowledged and no records are skipped.
